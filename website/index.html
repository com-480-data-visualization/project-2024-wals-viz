Official Languages

An Official Language is a language having certain rights to be used in defined situations. These rights can be created in written form or by historic usage. 178 countries recognize an official language, 101 of them recognizing more than one.

Language genealogy

Language genealogy is the study of the historical relationships between languages, tracing their evolution over time. Much like a family tree, languages can be grouped into families based on shared ancestry. For example, the Romance languages (Spanish, French, Italian, etc.) all descend from Latin.

This fascinating field illuminates the connections between cultures and the ways human communication has evolved.

Syntax Puzzles

Languages around the world present different ordering of the sentence elements, for example in English, the Subject comes first, followed by the Verb and the Object. Other languages may use other ways to compose a sentence.
